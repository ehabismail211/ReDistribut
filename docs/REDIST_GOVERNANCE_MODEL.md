# ReDist Governance Model

Date: 2026-06-21  
Status: Governance framework for pilot and pre-commercial launch  
Legal status: Not legal advice

## Purpose

This model defines who is responsible for decisions during the founder-guided UAE pilot and future commercial readiness work.

## Governance Principles

- Founder-guided until real workflows prove repeatability.
- No public self-serve launch until launch blockers are closed.
- No restricted categories without legal and operational approval.
- No certificate or impact claim should overstate what ReDist proves.
- Every Critical issue pauses the affected workflow.
- Commercial scale requires legal, privacy, support, and billing readiness.

## Founder Responsibilities

The founder is responsible for:

- Approving pilot participants.
- Approving safe categories for first workflows.
- Reviewing legal/privacy/commercial frameworks with counsel.
- Ensuring staging/prod access is protected appropriately.
- Owning participant communication.
- Approving first transfer completion decisions where needed.
- Reviewing certificates before external use during pilot.
- Maintaining issue log, tracking center, and decision gates.
- Deciding Continue / Expand / Pause.
- Preventing premature commercial or public launch.

Founder may delegate tasks, but not accountability for pilot safety and launch gates.

## Admin Responsibilities

Platform admins/reviewers are responsible for:

- Reviewing organization verification evidence.
- Applying category eligibility decisions.
- Monitoring issue/dispute queues.
- Handling certificate review workflows.
- Reviewing audit logs where needed.
- Escalating Critical/High issues.
- Avoiding unauthorized access to participant data.
- Recording admin decisions clearly.

Admin responsibilities require role-based access and audit logging.

## Organization Responsibilities

Participating organizations are responsible for:

- Assigning authorized users.
- Providing accurate organization information.
- Ensuring resource ownership/right to transfer.
- Listing only safe and approved resources.
- Providing accurate quantity, condition, location, and urgency.
- Reviewing requests responsibly.
- Confirming handover facts honestly.
- Reporting disputes or errors quickly.
- Keeping staging/commercial access private.
- Providing feedback during pilot.

## Pilot Governance

Pilot governance applies until the founder approves commercial conversion.

| Governance Area | Rule |
| --- | --- |
| Participant access | Invite-only |
| Category access | Founder-approved only |
| First workflow | Founder-guided |
| Certificate use | Review before external sharing |
| Impact claims | Estimates only unless validated |
| Issue handling | Log all issues; pause Critical workflows |
| Expansion | Requires first transfer evidence and decision gate update |
| Public launch | No-Go until legal/commercial blockers close |

## Decision Rights

| Decision | Owner | Required Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Invite participant | Founder | Fit, category, contact owner |
| Approve category | Founder / reviewer | Safety and verification context |
| Mark pilot workflow complete | Founder | Transfer, certificate, feedback evidence |
| Expand pilot | Founder | Decision gate and issue review |
| Suspend participant | Founder / admin | Issue or trust evidence |
| Publish case study | Founder + participant approval | Written approval |
| Launch paid plan | Founder | Commercial/legal readiness |
| Launch public self-serve | Founder + legal/ops approval | All blockers closed |

## Governance Cadence

| Cadence | Review |
| --- | --- |
| After each pilot session | Tracking center, issue log, feedback |
| After first transfer | Post-mortem and decision gate |
| Weekly during pilot | Success metrics, risks, open blockers |
| Before paid conversion | Legal/privacy/commercial readiness |
| Before enterprise sale | Contracts, support, security, governance |
