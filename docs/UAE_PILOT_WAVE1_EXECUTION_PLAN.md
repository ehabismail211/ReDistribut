# UAE Pilot Wave 1 Execution Plan

Date: 2026-06-21  
Pilot mode: Founder-guided UAE organization workflow  
Recommendation basis: `docs/UI_CORRECTION_SPRINT_1_REVALIDATION_REPORT.md`

## Objectives

Wave 1 should validate whether real UAE organizations can complete the core ReDist redistribution workflow with founder guidance:

1. Onboard and verify a small balanced cohort.
2. Create real surplus listings.
3. Discover resources from the recipient side.
4. Submit and approve requests.
5. Complete handovers.
6. Generate transfer certificates.
7. Review impact evidence and pilot feedback.

Wave 1 is not a public launch. It is a controlled operating test for the corrected workflow:

Dashboard -> Discover -> Requests -> Transfers -> Impact

## Scope

### Included

- 5 founder-selected UAE organizations.
- 2 supplier-heavy organizations.
- 2 recipient-heavy organizations.
- 1 mixed or ecosystem participant.
- Founder-guided onboarding and first workflow completion.
- Manual verification review where needed.
- Controlled categories only.
- Realistic listings, requests, handovers, certificates, and feedback.

### Excluded

- Public self-serve onboarding.
- Regulated, hazardous, medical, pharmaceutical, or controlled goods.
- Automated payments, escrow, tax invoicing, customs processing, or legal title transfer.
- Unguided multi-organization expansion.
- New product feature development during execution.

## Daily Execution Plan

### Day 0: Founder Preparation

- Confirm participant list and primary contacts.
- Confirm permitted categories and prohibited categories.
- Prepare support channel and escalation owner.
- Prepare observation sheet and go/no-go scorecard.
- Confirm test environment, account access, and known limitations.
- Prepare one sample workflow walkthrough for each participant type.

Output:

- Wave 1 participant list approved.
- Support owner assigned.
- Known limitations communicated internally.

### Day 1: Onboarding And Orientation

- Walk each organization through workspace entry.
- Explain the workflow spine: Dashboard, Discover, Requests, Transfers, Impact.
- Confirm role: supplier, recipient, or mixed.
- Confirm organization profile and verification expectations.
- Capture first confusion points.

Output:

- All 5 organizations oriented.
- First support issues logged.
- Any no-go verification or category risks escalated.

### Day 2: Supplier Listing Creation

- Guide suppliers through creating surplus listings.
- Confirm category, quantity, location, urgency, and handover method.
- Review listing copy for compliance and operational clarity.
- Confirm Discover displays resources correctly.

Output:

- At least 3 listings published.
- Urgent resources clearly visible.
- Supplier feedback captured.

### Day 3: Recipient Discovery And Request Submission

- Guide recipients through Discover.
- Have recipients filter by category, location, urgency, and pickup readiness.
- Submit requests against available resources.
- Record request quality and missing information.

Output:

- At least 4 requests submitted.
- Recipient confusion points logged.
- Supplier notification/queue behavior observed.

### Day 4: Approval And Handover Planning

- Guide suppliers through Requests.
- Approve or decline submitted requests.
- Confirm quantity reservation behavior.
- Move accepted requests into Transfers.
- Confirm handover location and method.

Output:

- At least 3 approved requests.
- No over-reservation observed.
- Handover plan confirmed for each active transfer.

### Day 5: Handover And Verification

- Conduct first real or controlled handovers.
- Use Transfers workspace to verify completion.
- Generate certificates for completed transfers.
- Review certificate data and QR verification path.

Output:

- At least 2 completed handovers.
- Certificates generated for each completed transfer.
- Certificate review feedback captured.

### Day 6: Impact Review And Feedback

- Review Impact page with participants.
- Explain pilot-based and completed-transfer metrics clearly.
- Capture feedback on ESG/results credibility.
- Capture support, trust, and workflow friction.

Output:

- Participant feedback collected.
- Impact interpretation issues logged.
- Pilot scorecard updated.

### Day 7: Wave 1 Review

- Review scorecard.
- Review unresolved issues by severity.
- Decide continue, pause, remediate, or expand.
- Prepare founder decision memo.

Output:

- Wave 1 Go / Conditional Go / No-Go decision.
- Remediation backlog limited to observed issues.
- Expansion decision documented.

## Operating Rules

1. Founder guides every first workflow.
2. No unmanaged public invites.
3. No restricted categories.
4. No transfer is considered complete until handover is verified.
5. Every completed handover must generate a certificate.
6. Every participant must provide feedback after first workflow.
7. Critical issues pause expansion immediately.
8. Enhancement requests are recorded, not built during Wave 1.

## Exit Criteria

Wave 1 can exit successfully when:

- 5 organizations have completed guided onboarding.
- At least 5 handovers are completed or the founder accepts a smaller evidence set due to participant availability.
- 100% of completed handovers have certificates.
- No Critical security, certificate, verification, or data isolation issues remain open.
- Founder confirms that the corrected workflow is understandable to real users.
- Go/no-go scorecard reaches at least Conditional Go.

## Wave 1 Recommendation

Conditional Go.

ReDist is ready for a first founder-guided UAE organization pilot wave. It is not yet ready for unmanaged self-serve onboarding.
