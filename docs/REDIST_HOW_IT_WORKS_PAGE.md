# ReDist How It Works Page

Date: 2026-06-22  
Status: Public page copy draft  
Audience: Suppliers, recipients, partners, and ESG stakeholders

## Page Headline

How ReDist turns surplus into verified redistribution.

## Intro Copy

ReDist gives verified organizations a structured way to list resources, discover available surplus, submit requests, coordinate handovers, verify completion, and track impact evidence.

The workflow is designed for operational clarity, not consumer shopping.

## Step 1: List

For suppliers:

Create a listing for surplus resources your organization is ready to redistribute.

Useful listing details include:

- Resource name.
- Quantity.
- Condition.
- Location.
- Availability or urgency.
- Handover notes.
- Supplier organization.

Supplier value:

- Keep control over what is shared.
- Review requests before committing.
- Make surplus visible to verified recipients.

## Step 2: Discover

For recipients:

Browse available resources from verified organizations.

Discovery focuses on practical sourcing decisions:

- What is available?
- Where is it?
- How urgent is it?
- What quantity is offered?
- Who is supplying it?
- Is it suitable for our organization?

Recipient value:

- Find available surplus without relying only on informal networks.
- Compare resources by category, location, urgency, and supplier context.

## Step 3: Request

Recipients submit a request for a resource.

A request can include:

- Requested quantity.
- Organization details.
- Intended use.
- Pickup or handover notes.
- Contact responsibility.

Supplier value:

- Review before approving.
- Approve, decline, or request clarification.
- Keep handover decisions controlled.

## Step 4: Transfer

After approval, the workflow moves toward handover.

Transfer coordination may include:

- Pickup or drop-off timing.
- Location details.
- Loading or access instructions.
- Quantity confirmation.
- Supplier and recipient next steps.

Important note:

ReDist does not currently act as a delivery provider, escrow provider, payment processor, or legal transfer authority.

## Step 5: Verify

After handover, completion is verified through the workflow.

Verification helps confirm:

- Supplier and recipient organizations.
- Resource transferred.
- Quantity.
- Handover completion status.
- Certificate readiness.

Certificate boundary:

ReDist certificates are operational evidence of a completed platform workflow. They are not legal guarantees, audited ESG certificates, safety approvals, or tax documents.

## Step 6: Impact

Completed transfers can contribute to impact visibility.

ReDist is designed to track:

- AED value recovered.
- Items redistributed.
- Waste diverted.
- Certificate evidence.
- Recipient usefulness.
- Environmental estimates where methodology supports them.

Impact boundary:

Impact claims should be based on completed transfers and clearly labeled estimates. ReDist will not publish pilot results without participant approval.

## Who This Is For

ReDist is designed for:

- Suppliers with surplus inventory, equipment, furniture, packaging, fixtures, or other reusable resources.
- Recipients such as NGOs, community organizations, schools, support centers, and approved organizational buyers.
- Partners who want to support circular economy workflows with practical evidence.
