# ReDist ESG Reporting Package

Date: 2026-06-21  
Status: Reporting package concept; impact numbers require methodology validation before external ESG claims

## Purpose

The ReDist ESG Reporting Package turns completed redistribution workflows into credible internal reporting evidence.

It should help organizations answer:

What value did we create by redistributing surplus resources instead of wasting or underusing them?

## Reporting Principles

- Report only completed or verified transfers.
- Separate estimates from audited facts.
- Tie every metric to transfer evidence where possible.
- Avoid vague marketing claims.
- Keep certificate references visible.
- Clearly label methodology assumptions.

## Core Metrics

### Waste Diverted

Definition:

Estimated kilograms of material kept from disposal through completed transfers.

Inputs:

- Resource category.
- Quantity.
- Unit type.
- Completed transfer status.
- Category-specific weight estimate, if available.

Reporting language:

> Estimated waste diverted from completed ReDist transfers.

Do not claim:

> Audited waste reduction.

Unless externally validated.

### CO2 Impact

Definition:

Estimated emissions avoided through redistribution activity.

Inputs:

- Resource category.
- Quantity.
- Waste diversion estimate.
- CO2 factor assumption.

Reporting language:

> Estimated CO2 impact based on completed transfer activity and ReDist's pilot calculation assumptions.

Do not claim:

> Certified carbon offset.

Unless a validated carbon methodology and external verification exist.

### AED Recovered

Definition:

Estimated economic value retained or redirected through completed redistribution.

Inputs:

- Resource quantity.
- Unit value estimate.
- Completed transfer status.
- Category or listing value assumptions.

Reporting language:

> Estimated AED value recovered through redistribution.

Do not claim:

> Recognized accounting value, donation deduction, or tax value.

Unless finance/legal review confirms it.

### Transfer Verification

Definition:

Evidence that a transfer was recorded as completed in ReDist.

Inputs:

- Transfer/request reference.
- Supplier organization.
- Recipient organization.
- Resource details.
- Quantity.
- Completion date.
- Certificate reference.
- QR verification token.

Reporting language:

> Transfer verification confirms that ReDist recorded the handover as completed.

Do not claim:

> Legal title transfer, payment receipt, tax invoice, or regulatory clearance.

## Executive ESG Reporting Concepts

### Monthly Executive Snapshot

Recommended sections:

- Total AED recovered.
- Total items/resources redistributed.
- Estimated waste diverted.
- Estimated CO2 impact.
- Completed transfers.
- Certificates generated.
- Top categories.
- Top locations.
- Supplier contribution.
- Recipient contribution.
- Open issues and trust notes.

### Quarterly Impact Review

Recommended sections:

- Executive summary.
- Transfer evidence summary.
- Category breakdown.
- Location breakdown.
- Certificate appendix.
- Methodology assumptions.
- Data quality notes.
- Recommendations for next quarter.

### Certificate Evidence Appendix

Fields:

- Certificate ID.
- Transfer reference.
- Supplier.
- Recipient.
- Resource.
- Quantity.
- Date.
- QR verification link/token.
- Impact snapshot.

## Methodology Disclaimer

All pilot impact metrics should include:

> Impact values are estimated from completed ReDist transfer records using pilot-stage calculation assumptions. They are intended for operational insight and internal review unless separately validated for external ESG reporting.

## Readiness Requirements Before Selling ESG Package

Required before commercial sale:

- Founder-approved impact methodology note.
- Certificate disclaimer.
- Data quality review.
- External-claims policy.
- Example report with real pilot data.
- Approval workflow for public use of participant names or quotes.
