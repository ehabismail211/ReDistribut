# ReDist Discovery Question Library

Target use: founder meetings, qualification calls, pilot selection, and partner discovery  
Question count: 112

## A. Organization Discovery

1. What is your organization’s primary activity?
2. Which emirate or city does your team operate in?
3. What locations, branches, or warehouses would be involved?
4. Who owns sustainability, operations, procurement, or community impact decisions?
5. Who would be the practical day-to-day coordinator?
6. What resource categories are relevant to your organization?
7. What operational constraints affect redistribution?
8. What approvals are needed before joining a pilot?
9. Does your organization have existing donation or redistribution processes?
10. What tools do you currently use to coordinate surplus or resource needs?
11. What has worked well in your existing process?
12. What has failed or created friction?
13. What information do you need before trusting another organization?
14. What information should another organization know about you?

## B. Supplier Discovery

15. Do you currently have usable surplus?
16. What categories of surplus appear most often?
17. What is the most realistic first pilot item?
18. How often does surplus become available?
19. Who decides whether surplus can be released?
20. Who physically controls the surplus location?
21. What quantity is available for a first pilot?
22. What condition information can you provide?
23. Are photos or supporting details available?
24. Is there an expiry, storage, or pickup deadline?
25. Is pickup, delivery, or courier coordination preferred?
26. Are there access restrictions at the handover site?
27. What recipient types are acceptable?
28. What recipient types are not acceptable?
29. What compliance restrictions apply?
30. Are there brand, confidentiality, or reputational concerns?
31. Do you need management approval for each transfer?
32. What evidence do you need after completion?
33. Would certificate-backed evidence be useful internally?
34. What would make the supplier workflow successful?
35. What would make it too risky?

## C. Recipient Discovery

36. What resource need is most urgent?
37. What resources would be useful in the next 30 days?
38. What quantity do you need?
39. What condition is acceptable?
40. Who will use or benefit from the resource?
41. Where would the resource be received or stored?
42. Can your team collect resources?
43. Can your team receive delivery?
44. Who can approve requests internally?
45. Who can confirm receipt?
46. What handover timing works best?
47. What categories are unsuitable or risky?
48. What documentation do you need after receipt?
49. Would certificates help your internal reporting?
50. What would make a supplier trustworthy?
51. What would prevent you from accepting a resource?
52. Can you provide feedback after the first workflow?
53. Can you complete one guided request in 30 days?

## D. Strategic Partner Discovery

54. What type of partnership are you exploring?
55. Do you have access to suppliers, recipients, or both?
56. How large is your organization network?
57. Which sectors are strongest in your network?
58. Can you introduce pilot-ready organizations?
59. Would you participate directly or refer others?
60. What role would your organization want in the pilot?
61. Do you have ESG or circular economy objectives?
62. Would a case study be valuable if approved?
63. What communications approval is required?
64. What public visibility is acceptable?
65. Can you support evidence standards?
66. Can you help validate safe categories?
67. Can you support founder introductions?
68. What would make this partnership valuable in 90 days?

## E. ESG Discovery

69. What ESG or sustainability goals are currently active?
70. Do you track waste diversion today?
71. Do you track recovered value today?
72. Do you track social or recipient usefulness?
73. What evidence do ESG stakeholders require?
74. What data cannot be estimated without audit?
75. What environmental assumptions would require approval?
76. Would certificate references support internal reporting?
77. Who reviews ESG claims before publication?
78. What impact language is acceptable?
79. What impact language should be avoided?
80. Would a pilot case study help future ESG reporting?
81. What would make ReDist credible to your ESG team?
82. What would create ESG or reputational risk?

## F. Pilot Qualification

83. Can the organization commit to one guided workflow?
84. Can the organization assign one responsible contact?
85. Is there a clear decision maker?
86. Is the first category low-risk?
87. Is the first resource or need clearly defined?
88. Can the organization respond within 48 hours during the pilot?
89. Can handover happen in a controlled location?
90. Can participants provide feedback?
91. Can screenshots or evidence be collected with permission?
92. Can the organization avoid sensitive documents in public forms?
93. Is there any legal, compliance, or safety blocker?
94. Is a founder-guided workflow acceptable?

## G. Risk Assessment

95. Could the resource be unsafe, regulated, or restricted?
96. Could the transfer create liability concerns?
97. Could storage, pickup, or timing fail?
98. Could participants disagree about condition?
99. Could brand reputation be affected?
100. Could there be confidentiality concerns?
101. Could there be data privacy concerns?
102. Could payment, tax, customs, or title transfer be misunderstood?
103. Could the organization publish claims without approval?
104. What would require pausing the pilot?

## H. Growth Opportunity

105. If the first workflow succeeds, what category comes next?
106. How many branches or locations could participate later?
107. How often could surplus or requests occur monthly?
108. Which internal team would own ongoing use?
109. Would a paid tier be considered after evidence is proven?
110. What integrations or reports would matter later?
111. Who else should ReDist speak with?
112. What would make ReDist a long-term partner?

## Arabic Use Note

Use these questions in Arabic during meetings when needed. Keep the English record as the source of truth, and add Arabic notes directly underneath each answer when the conversation is bilingual.
