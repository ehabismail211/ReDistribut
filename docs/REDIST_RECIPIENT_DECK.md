# ReDist Recipient Deck

Purpose: recipient-focused meeting presentation for NGOs, schools, charities, community organizations, training centers, and approved recipients.  
Slide count: 12.  
Recommended duration: 18-22 minutes plus 10 minutes discovery.  
QR destination: `https://www.redistribut.com`

## Deck Design

- Cover uses ReDist wordmark and recipient-specific title.
- Use brand-700 for action labels: Discover, Request, Track, Receive, Verify.
- Use screenshots: Discover resources, submit request, requests queue, certificates, impact.
- Place QR on slides 1 and 12.
- Keep language practical and avoid promising guaranteed supply.

## Slide 1: Cover

Title: ReDist For Recipients  
Subtitle: Discover available surplus resources through a controlled organization workflow.  
Image placeholder: Discover resources screenshot.  
QR: bottom-right.

## Slide 2: Recipient Problem

Layout: problem list with simple visual.

Challenges:

- Resource needs are real but supply is hard to find.
- Informal messages do not provide enough detail.
- Handover expectations can be unclear.
- Evidence after receipt is limited.

## Slide 3: Recipient Value

Layout: four value cards.

ReDist helps recipients:

- Discover available resources.
- Understand quantity, condition, location, and urgency.
- Submit structured requests.
- Track status through handover.
- Review certificate evidence after completion.

## Slide 4: Recipient Workflow

Layout: flow ribbon.

Discover -> Review fit -> Submit request -> Track supplier response -> Coordinate handover -> Verify receipt -> Review evidence

## Slide 5: Discover Resources

Layout: screenshot-led slide.

Recipients can review:

- Resource name.
- Quantity.
- Supplier.
- Location.
- Condition.
- Urgency.
- Handover readiness.

Image placeholder: workspace Discover screenshot.

## Slide 6: Submit A Strong Request

Layout: request form example.

A good request includes:

- Required quantity.
- Why the resource is needed.
- Pickup or receipt ability.
- Timing constraints.
- Responsible contact.

## Slide 7: Track Status

Layout: queue visualization.

Statuses:

- Awaiting supplier response.
- Approved.
- Awaiting handover.
- Verification required.
- Completed or closed.

## Slide 8: Certificate And Impact Evidence

Layout: certificate and impact cards.

Recipients can use evidence for:

- Internal records.
- Donor or board updates.
- Operational transparency.
- Future impact stories with permission.

Boundary:

Certificates are not legal or audited ESG documents.

## Slide 9: What Recipients Can Request First

Layout: safe category examples.

Preferred categories:

- Furniture and fixtures.
- Community supplies.
- Packaging materials.
- Pallets and cartons.
- Non-sensitive equipment.
- Training supplies.

Excluded unless reviewed:

- Medical or pharmaceutical items.
- Hazardous goods.
- Controlled goods.
- Payment, customs, or title-transfer workflows.

## Slide 10: Recipient Qualification

Layout: checklist.

Required:

- Real resource need.
- Coordinator.
- Decision maker.
- Collection or receipt capacity.
- Feedback readiness.
- Need matches low-risk category.

## Slide 11: Recipient FAQ

Layout: five Q&A tiles.

- Can individuals request resources? No, organizations only.
- Are resources guaranteed? No.
- Do recipients pay? Not in Wave 1.
- Can partial quantities be requested? Discuss during guided pilot.
- Can certificates support reporting? Yes as operational evidence, not audited ESG.

## Slide 12: Next Step

Layout: close slide with QR and action list.

Next step:

1. Confirm first resource need.
2. Assign responsible contact.
3. Confirm pickup or receipt capacity.
4. Schedule recipient onboarding.

QR: `https://www.redistribut.com`

## Presentation Flow

Need -> discovery -> request workflow -> handover tracking -> evidence -> qualification -> onboarding.

## Estimated Meeting Duration

- 12-minute recipient teaser: slides 1-5 and 12.
- 20-minute recipient meeting: full deck.
- 30-minute qualification session: full deck plus checklist discussion.
