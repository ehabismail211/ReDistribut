# UAE Pilot First Transfer Walkthrough

Date: 2026-06-21  
Pilot mode: Founder-guided UAE Pilot Wave 1  
Primary sequence: `UAE-W1-001` supplier -> `UAE-W1-002` recipient

## Purpose

This walkthrough guides the first real supplier-recipient workflow from surplus listing to impact evidence.

Do not invite additional organizations until this first workflow is completed, blocked with a clear reason, or explicitly deferred by the founder.

## Participants

| Role | Participant | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Supplier | `UAE-W1-001` Restaurant supplier | Create first surplus listing and review request |
| Recipient | `UAE-W1-002` NGO recipient | Discover resource, submit request, confirm receipt |
| Founder | ReDist founder | Guide workflow, observe friction, verify certificate and impact evidence |

## Step 1: Listing Creation

Owner: Supplier with founder guidance

Goal: Create one accurate surplus resource listing.

Supplier should confirm:

- Resource name.
- Category.
- Quantity and unit.
- Condition.
- City and pickup/handover location.
- Expiry or urgency, if relevant.
- Pickup readiness.
- Any handling or safety notes.

Founder observes:

- Can supplier find the listing action from Dashboard?
- Is listing language operational rather than marketplace-like?
- Does supplier know what information recipients need?
- Does the listing feel safe to publish?

Pass criteria:

- Listing is published or ready for recipient discovery.
- No restricted category concern.
- Quantity and handover details are clear.

## Step 2: Discovery

Owner: Recipient with founder guidance

Goal: Recipient finds the supplier's available resource.

Recipient should review:

- Resource name.
- Quantity.
- Condition.
- Location.
- Urgency.
- Supplier organization.
- Handover suitability.

Founder observes:

- Can recipient find Discover from Dashboard?
- Are filters understandable?
- Is urgency visible?
- Is supplier context clear enough to trust?
- Does recipient know whether the resource is suitable?

Pass criteria:

- Recipient can identify the resource.
- Recipient understands practical handover information.
- Recipient can decide whether to request.

## Step 3: Request

Owner: Recipient

Goal: Submit one clear resource request.

Request should include:

- Requested quantity.
- Intended use or recipient need.
- Pickup/receipt readiness.
- Any timing constraints.
- Contact or coordination note, if available in workflow.

Founder observes:

- Does recipient understand what they are asking for?
- Is any required request information missing?
- Does recipient understand what happens next?

Pass criteria:

- Request is submitted.
- Supplier can understand and act on it.
- Request appears in the supplier's Requests queue.

## Step 4: Approval

Owner: Supplier

Goal: Supplier reviews and accepts, declines, or requests clarification.

Supplier should review:

- Requesting organization.
- Requested quantity.
- Resource/listing name.
- City/location.
- Current status.
- Next required action.

Founder observes:

- Can supplier find the request?
- Is the next action obvious?
- Does approval feel safe and reversible enough for pilot use?
- If declined, is the reason clear?

Pass criteria:

- Request receives a clear decision.
- Accepted request moves toward Transfers.
- Declined request is understandable and logged.

## Step 5: Handover

Owner: Supplier and recipient

Goal: Complete the operational handover.

Confirm before handover:

- Quantity.
- Location.
- Time window.
- Responsible contact.
- Any handling requirements.
- Evidence expectation, if applicable.

Founder observes:

- Does ReDist provide enough context for coordination?
- Does either side need information that was not captured?
- Is mobile access needed at handover time?

Pass criteria:

- Both sides confirm handover occurred or clearly explain why it did not.
- Quantity discrepancy, if any, is logged.
- No safety or trust issue emerges.

## Step 6: Verification

Owner: Founder-guided supplier/recipient confirmation

Goal: Mark the transfer as verified or completed according to the existing workflow.

Verify:

- Correct request/transfer reference.
- Correct supplier.
- Correct recipient.
- Correct resource.
- Correct quantity.
- Correct completion status.

Founder observes:

- Is the verification action visible?
- Does the participant understand who must confirm completion?
- Are completed and pending transfers clearly separated?

Pass criteria:

- Transfer is marked complete.
- Any audit or status change is visible.
- No unauthorized participant can complete the wrong transfer.

## Step 7: Certificate

Owner: Founder and participants

Goal: Generate and review certificate evidence for the completed transfer.

Review:

- Certificate reference.
- Supplier organization.
- Recipient organization.
- Resource/listing name.
- Quantity.
- Completion date.
- QR verification path.
- Public-safe verification output.

Founder observes:

- Does certificate data match the real handover?
- Does the QR/public verification path make sense?
- Would supplier or recipient use this evidence internally?

Pass criteria:

- Certificate is generated.
- Certificate information is accurate.
- QR verification works.
- Both participants understand what the certificate proves and does not prove.

## Step 8: Impact Update

Owner: Founder with participant review

Goal: Review how the completed transfer updates impact evidence.

Review:

- AED value recovered.
- Items redistributed.
- Waste diverted.
- CO2/environmental estimate, if shown.
- Completed handover/certificate evidence.

Founder observes:

- Does impact feel credible?
- Are estimates clearly understood as estimates?
- Does the participant need export/reporting support later?

Pass criteria:

- Impact page reflects completed activity or founder explains timing/limitations.
- Participant understands certificate-backed evidence.
- Feedback is captured.

## Stop Conditions

Pause the workflow if:

- A restricted or unsafe category appears.
- Participant sees data from another organization unexpectedly.
- Certificate data is wrong.
- Handover cannot be confirmed.
- Participant cannot proceed without a new product feature.
- Founder cannot explain a trust or safety concern clearly.
