# ReDist Customer Support Playbook

Date: 2026-06-21  
Status: Founder-operated support playbook  
Owner: Founder during pilot; future Customer Support / Operations Lead

## Purpose

This playbook helps ReDist support suppliers, recipients, and admins during pilot and early commercial use.

## Support Principles

- Respond calmly and operationally.
- Clarify the workflow step before giving instructions.
- Record every blocker.
- Escalate trust, safety, certificate, privacy, and data issues immediately.
- Do not promise legal, tax, ESG, or safety guarantees.
- Do not build features during live support; log requests first.

## Support Channels

Pilot channels:

- Founder email.
- Founder phone/WhatsApp.
- Scheduled onboarding calls.
- Issue log.

Future channels:

- Support inbox.
- Help desk/ticket system.
- Status page.
- Customer success check-ins.

## Supplier Support

Common supplier questions:

- How do I create a listing?
- What information should a listing include?
- Can I decline a request?
- How do I approve a request?
- What happens after acceptance?
- How is handover verified?
- What does the certificate prove?

Support approach:

1. Identify listing/request reference.
2. Confirm supplier organization and user role.
3. Confirm category is allowed.
4. Confirm quantity, condition, location, urgency.
5. Guide next action.
6. Record confusion point if founder guidance was needed.

Escalate if:

- Supplier wants to list restricted goods.
- Supplier disputes recipient suitability.
- Supplier cannot confirm ownership/right to transfer.
- Certificate data appears wrong.

## Recipient Support

Common recipient questions:

- How do I find resources?
- Can I request a partial quantity?
- Why was my request declined?
- How do I track status?
- What should I confirm at handover?
- How do I access certificate evidence?

Support approach:

1. Identify resource/listing.
2. Confirm recipient organization and role.
3. Confirm requested quantity and intended use.
4. Explain supplier approval step.
5. Explain transfer and certificate path.
6. Record feedback after first workflow.

Escalate if:

- Recipient reports resource condition mismatch.
- Recipient disputes supplier action.
- Recipient cannot access certificate.
- Recipient needs legal/tax proof beyond ReDist scope.

## Verification Questions

Common questions:

- What documents are needed?
- Why is verification pending?
- Why was a document rejected?
- Can we proceed before full verification?
- What categories require extra approval?

Support approach:

- Explain verification as trust context, not guarantee.
- Keep restricted categories disabled until approved.
- Request clear updated evidence where needed.
- Record reviewer decision and notes.

Escalate if:

- Document authenticity is unclear.
- Category requires legal review.
- Participant disputes rejection.
- Sensitive documents were shared through unsafe channel.

## Certificate Questions

Common questions:

- What is a certificate?
- Is it legally binding?
- Can we share it?
- Why is certificate missing?
- Why does QR verification show limited data?
- What if certificate data is wrong?

Support response:

- Certificate is platform evidence of a recorded ReDist transfer.
- It is not a tax invoice, payment receipt, customs document, government certificate, or legal title document.
- QR verification is public-safe and intentionally limited.
- Data disputes should pause external sharing until reviewed.

Escalate if:

- Certificate data mismatch.
- QR verification failure.
- Participant wants external/public use.
- Certificate is disputed.

## Escalation Matrix

| Issue | Severity | Owner | Response Target |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cross-tenant data exposure | Critical | Engineering + Founder | Immediate |
| Private document exposure | Critical | Engineering + Founder | Immediate |
| Unsafe/restricted resource | Critical | Founder / Trust & Safety | Immediate |
| Certificate mismatch | High | Founder + Engineering | Same business day |
| Failed handover | High | Founder / Ops | Same business day |
| Verification dispute | High | Founder / Reviewer | Same business day |
| User blocked from workflow | Medium | Support / Ops | 2 business days |
| Copy or training confusion | Low | Support / Ops | Weekly review |

## Support Closeout

Every support case should end with:

- Issue summary.
- Participant affected.
- Workflow area.
- Severity.
- Resolution or workaround.
- Next owner.
- Tracking update.
