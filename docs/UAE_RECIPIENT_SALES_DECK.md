# UAE Recipient Sales Deck

Date: 2026-06-21  
Audience: NGOs, charities, community organizations, schools, and approved recipient partners  
Use: Founder-led intro calls before UAE Pilot Wave 1

## Slide 1: ReDist For Recipients

ReDist helps verified recipient organizations discover available surplus resources, submit requests, complete handovers, and receive certificate-backed impact evidence.

The first UAE pilot is private, founder-guided, and limited to approved organizations.

## Slide 2: NGO / Charity Value Proposition

Recipient organizations often need practical resources but rely on informal networks to find them.

ReDist helps recipients:

- See available surplus resources from approved suppliers.
- Request resources through a structured workflow.
- Understand quantity, condition, location, and urgency before asking.
- Track whether a request is pending, approved, declined, or transferred.
- Confirm handover completion.
- Receive certificate evidence after completed transfers.
- Review impact created through redistribution.

## Slide 3: The Problem For Recipients

Common recipient challenges:

- Available resources are hard to discover.
- Supplier information may be incomplete.
- Requests happen through informal messages.
- Handover expectations can be unclear.
- There is limited evidence after receiving resources.
- Impact reporting is difficult without transfer records.
- Trust depends on personal contacts rather than a repeatable process.

## Slide 4: The ReDist Request Workflow

Recipient workflow:

1. Open Dashboard.
2. Go to Discover.
3. Review available resources by category, quantity, location, and urgency.
4. Select a suitable resource.
5. Submit a request.
6. Track request status.
7. Coordinate handover if approved.
8. Confirm receipt or verification step.
9. Review certificate and impact evidence.
10. Submit pilot feedback.

## Slide 5: What Recipients Can Request In Wave 1

Preferred first pilot categories:

- Prepared or packaged food where safe and permitted.
- Furniture and fixtures.
- Community supplies.
- Packaging materials.
- Pallets, cartons, and operational supplies.

Excluded from first pilot unless separately approved:

- Medical or pharmaceutical items.
- Hazardous materials.
- Controlled goods.
- Items without clear supplier ownership.
- Requests requiring payment, tax, customs, or legal title transfer.

## Slide 6: Trust Model

ReDist is designed around trust and controlled participation.

Trust controls include:

- Invite-only pilot access.
- Organization-level accounts.
- Founder-guided onboarding.
- Verification and document review where needed.
- Supplier and recipient context.
- Request approval by the supplier.
- Handover verification.
- Transfer certificates.
- Public-safe QR verification.
- Issue logging and pilot controls.

## Slide 7: Why Certificates Matter

After a completed transfer, ReDist can generate a certificate that records:

- Supplier organization.
- Recipient organization.
- Resource summary.
- Quantity.
- Transfer date.
- Location.
- Impact snapshot.
- QR verification token.

The certificate is not a legal document or invoice. It is platform evidence that the transfer was recorded as completed.

## Slide 10: FAQ

### Is ReDist only for charities?

No. NGOs and charities can participate, but ReDist supports verified organizations across the redistribution ecosystem.

### Can individuals request resources?

Not in Wave 1. The pilot is organization-to-organization only.

### Do we pay for resources?

The first pilot focuses on controlled redistribution workflow validation. Payment workflows are not part of Wave 1.

### How do we know suppliers are real?

The pilot is invite-only and founder-guided, with organization context and verification controls.

### Can we choose what to request?

Yes, within available approved resources and pilot-safe categories.

### What if a request is declined?

Declines are part of the workflow. The supplier may decline because of quantity, timing, suitability, or operational constraints.

### What happens after receiving resources?

The transfer is verified, a certificate can be generated, impact is reviewed, and feedback is captured.

### What is the next step?

Book a 10-20 minute founder call to confirm your resource needs, pilot contact, and pickup/receipt readiness.
