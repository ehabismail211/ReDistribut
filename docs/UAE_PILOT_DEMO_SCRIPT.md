# UAE Pilot Demo Script

Date: 2026-06-20  
Audience: UAE pilot prospects  
Mode: Controlled, invite-only pilot  
Positioning: Enterprise circular economy operating platform

## Demo Goal

Show how ReDist helps verified organizations move surplus resources through a controlled workflow:

1. Create a listing.
2. Discover available resources.
3. Request an item.
4. Validate verification and trust.
5. Complete handover.
6. Review impact.
7. Generate a transfer certificate.
8. Verify the certificate through QR.

Do not present ReDist as a public marketplace or charity platform.

## Demo Setup

Recommended seeded flow:

- Sender: Restaurant A
- Receiver: NGO A
- Resource: Prepared meals or packaged food surplus
- Location: Dubai
- Outcome: Completed transfer with certificate and QR verification

Before the demo:

1. Confirm live staging validation passed.
2. Use seeded pilot accounts only.
3. Avoid real private documents.
4. Avoid promising production guarantees.
5. Explain that the pilot is founder-guided and invite-only.

## Opening Positioning

Use this opening:

> ReDist is a UAE-first circular economy platform for verified organizations. It helps teams identify surplus, request resources, complete controlled handovers, and produce auditable proof through impact tracking and QR-verifiable transfer certificates.

Avoid saying:

- "This is a marketplace."
- "This is a charity app."
- "Anyone can list anything."
- "The certificate is a legal/tax document."

## 1. 5-Minute Demo

Purpose: Quick executive overview for a founder, sustainability lead, operations manager, or restaurant/hotel decision maker.

### Timing

| Time | Segment | What To Show |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 0:00-0:45 | Positioning | ReDist purpose, controlled UAE pilot, verified organizations |
| 0:45-1:30 | Listing creation | Restaurant A creates or reviews a surplus listing |
| 1:30-2:10 | Discovery | NGO A discovers the listing and sees verification/trust context |
| 2:10-2:50 | Request workflow | NGO A requests quantity; Restaurant A accepts |
| 2:50-3:30 | Verification and trust | Show verification badge, trust score, and role-controlled access |
| 3:30-4:20 | Impact and certificate | Show impact update and transfer certificate |
| 4:20-5:00 | QR verification | Open public QR verification and close with pilot ask |

### Talk Track

1. "This is an invite-only UAE pilot for verified organizations."
2. "Restaurant A can publish surplus with quantity, category, location, and handover details."
3. "NGO A can discover approved resources and submit a controlled request."
4. "The workflow is permissioned: organization users, admins, reviewers, and founder roles are separated."
5. "Trust and verification are visible before the handover."
6. "After completion, ReDist updates impact and generates a QR-verifiable transfer certificate."
7. "For the pilot, we are asking each organization to complete one guided workflow and give feedback within 48 hours."

### Close

Ask:

> Is there one real surplus or resource need in your organization that we could test in a founder-guided pilot?

## 2. 10-Minute Demo

Purpose: Practical walkthrough for operations, sustainability, procurement, or NGO program users.

### Timing

| Time | Segment | What To Show |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 0:00-1:00 | Pilot framing | Invite-only, UAE-first, verified organizations, founder-guided |
| 1:00-2:15 | Organization and verification | Organization profile, verification status, badge |
| 2:15-3:30 | Listing creation | Category, quantity, unit, condition, location, handover method |
| 3:30-4:45 | Discovery | Search/filter, listing cards, verification/trust badge |
| 4:45-5:45 | Request workflow | Request quantity, message, acceptance, over-allocation prevention |
| 5:45-6:45 | Trust score | Trust badge, trust explanation, verification contribution |
| 6:45-7:45 | Impact dashboard | AED recovered, resources redistributed, waste prevented, CO2 saved |
| 7:45-9:00 | Transfer certificate | Certificate metadata, sender/receiver, item, impact/trust snapshot |
| 9:00-10:00 | QR verification and pilot ask | Public-safe QR page and next step |

### Talk Track

#### Pilot Framing

"The pilot is intentionally controlled. We want to validate real workflows with a small number of UAE organizations before opening the platform more widely."

#### Verification

"Verification makes this suitable for organizations, not anonymous public exchange. Depending on the organization type, we can review trade license, food permit, storage permit, NGO license, or equivalent manual evidence."

#### Listing Creation

"A listing captures enough operational detail to make the resource usable: what it is, quantity, unit, location, category, and handover method. The pilot avoids sensitive or restricted categories unless explicitly approved."

#### Discovery

"Discovery is not just browsing. The receiver can see trust and verification context so they know who they are dealing with before making a request."

#### Request Workflow

"The requester asks for a specific quantity. The sender controls acceptance. Quantity controls protect against over-allocation."

#### Trust Score

"Trust score is a platform signal based on verification, transaction behavior, response reliability, audit events, profile completeness, and document status."

#### Impact Dashboard

"Impact is calculated from completed workflows. The first pilot metrics focus on AED recovered, resources redistributed, waste prevented, CO2 saved, completed transactions, active listings, and active requests."

#### Transfer Certificate

"Every completed transfer can generate a certificate. It includes public-safe transfer proof: sender, receiver, resource, quantity, date, impact summary, trust snapshot, and QR token."

#### QR Verification

"When the QR is scanned, the public page shows only safe transfer facts. It does not expose private notes, documents, internal audit logs, or sensitive contact information."

### Close

Ask:

1. "Where would this fit your current process?"
2. "What proof would your team need after a handover?"
3. "What would stop you from completing one guided pilot workflow?"

### Discovery Questions

Ask before showing the product:

1. "What surplus resources do you most often have or need?"
2. "How do you handle those resources today?"
3. "Who approves giving or receiving items?"
4. "What proof do you need after a handover?"
5. "What would make this unsafe or difficult for your team?"

### Full Demo Narrative

#### Roles and Permissions

"For the pilot, access is role-based. Founder and platform admin users can monitor the pilot. Reviewers handle verification. Organization admins manage their own organization. Organization users have limited workflow access. Cross-organization access is blocked."

Show:

- Founder dashboard access.
- Reviewer access boundaries.
- Organization admin/user distinction.

#### Verification

"Verification is the trust foundation. A restaurant may need trade license and food permit. A warehouse may need trade license and storage permit. An NGO may need NGO license or founder-approved equivalent."

Show:

- Current verification level.
- Verification badge.
- Document status.
- Review history if available.

#### Listing Creation

"The sender creates a structured listing. This is not a casual post. It is operational data for a controlled transfer."

Show:

- Title.
- Category.
- Quantity and unit.
- Location.
- Handover method.
- Availability.

#### Discovery

"Receivers discover resources with context, not just inventory. Trust and verification are visible at the point of decision."

Show:

- Search/filter.
- Category filter.
- Listing cards.
- Verification and trust badges.

#### Request Workflow

"The receiver requests a quantity. The sender accepts or declines. Quantity controls prevent more being promised than exists."

Show:

- Request creation.
- Request status.
- Acceptance path.
- Over-allocation prevention if relevant.

#### Handover

"The first pilot handover is founder-assisted. We care less about volume and more about whether the workflow matches real operations."

Show:

- Completed transaction status.
- Handover details.
- Audit trail if available.

#### Trust Score

"Trust score makes operational reliability visible. It is not a social rating. It reflects verification, completion behavior, response time, acceptance, cancellations, disputes, audit events, profile completeness, and document health."

Show:

- Trust score card.
- Trust badge.
- Score explanation panel.

#### Impact Dashboard

"Impact turns completed workflows into measurable value: AED recovered, resources redistributed, waste prevented, estimated CO2 saved, completed transactions, active listings, and active requests."

Show:

- Organization impact.
- Monthly summary.
- Category/location breakdown if available.

#### Transfer Certificate

"The certificate is the auditable proof of a completed redistribution transfer. It is immutable after generation and safe to verify publicly through QR."

Show:

- Certificate ID.
- Sender and receiver.
- Item summary.
- Quantity.
- Transfer date.
- Trust snapshot.
- Impact snapshot.

Clarify:

"This certificate is not a tax invoice, payment receipt, customs document, or legal title document."

#### QR Verification

"The QR page shows public-safe facts only. It proves the certificate is valid without exposing private documents, contact details, internal notes, or audit logs."

Show:

- Valid status.
- Certificate ID.
- Resource summary.
- Sender.
- Receiver.
- Transfer date.

### Pilot Commitment Close

Ask:

1. "What would be the first real resource you would list or request?"
2. "Who should be the organization admin?"
3. "Can we complete one guided workflow in the next two weeks?"
4. "Will you provide feedback within 48 hours?"

## Objection Handling During Demo

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Is this a marketplace?" | "No. The pilot is controlled, verified, and organization-led. Discovery exists, but trust, verification, handover, impact, and certificates are the core." |
| "Is this a charity platform?" | "No. NGOs may participate, but ReDist is an enterprise circular economy platform for resource redistribution and operational proof." |
| "Can anyone see our data?" | "No. Organization data is tenant-scoped, role-based, and cross-organization access is blocked." |
| "Is the certificate legal proof?" | "It is platform proof of a recorded ReDist transfer. It is not a tax invoice, payment receipt, customs document, or title document." |
| "Can we use restricted categories?" | "Only with founder approval and the right verification. The first pilot avoids high-risk categories." |

## Demo Success Criteria

The demo succeeds if the prospect can answer:

1. What first workflow they would test.
2. Who owns approval.
3. What verification they can provide.
4. Whether certificate proof is useful.
5. Whether they can complete one guided workflow in two weeks.

## Follow-Up After Demo

Send:

1. Thank-you note.
2. Summary of first use case.
3. Required verification checklist.
4. Pilot expectations.
5. Proposed onboarding date.

Do not send credentials until the prospect is qualified and founder-approved.
