# UAE Pilot FAQ

Date: 2026-06-20  
Audience: UAE pilot prospects and founder-led demos  
Scope: Controlled, invite-only pilot

## Positioning

### 1. What is ReDist?

ReDist is a UAE-first circular economy platform for verified organizations to redistribute surplus resources through controlled workflows, trust signals, impact tracking, and QR-verifiable transfer certificates.

### 2. Is ReDist a marketplace?

No. ReDist includes discovery and listings, but the core product is an enterprise workflow for verified redistribution, handover control, trust, impact, and transfer proof.

### 3. Is ReDist a charity platform?

No. NGOs can participate, but ReDist is not charity-only. It supports restaurants, hotels, warehouses, suppliers, NGOs, and other organizations that need accountable redistribution workflows.

### 4. Why is ReDist UAE-first?

The UAE pilot lets ReDist validate organization verification, local operating expectations, Arabic/RTL readiness, trust, impact, and certificate workflows in one focused launch market before expanding.

### 5. What problem does ReDist solve?

Organizations often have surplus resources or resource needs but lack a trusted, auditable, and structured way to redistribute them. ReDist provides visibility, permissioned workflows, verification, impact metrics, and proof of completed transfers.

## Pilot Scope

### 6. What is the pilot objective?

The objective is to validate one real workflow per organization: setup, verification, listing or request, handover, certificate, impact review, and feedback.

### 7. Who can join the pilot?

Only invited UAE organizations. The first target cohort is two restaurants, one hotel, one warehouse, and one NGO.

### 8. How long is the pilot?

The controlled pilot is planned for 4-8 weeks, depending on adoption, feedback, bugs, and workflow completion.

### 9. What is expected from a pilot organization?

Each organization should assign an owner/admin, complete setup, provide verification evidence, run one guided workflow, review the certificate, and submit feedback within 48 hours.

### 10. Is there a cost for the pilot?

The initial controlled pilot is focused on validation. Commercial terms should be discussed only after the workflow proves useful.

## Users And Roles

### 11. What user roles exist?

Pilot roles include Founder, Platform Admin, Reviewer, Pilot Coordinator, Organization Admin, and Organization User.

### 12. What can an Organization Admin do?

An Organization Admin manages organization setup, verification, listings, request handling, handovers, and organization-level feedback.

### 13. What can an Organization User do?

An Organization User has limited access for discovery, requests, and assigned workflow participation. They should not have broad admin privileges.

### 14. Can one user access another organization?

No. Cross-organization access is blocked. Tenant isolation is a core pilot control.

### 15. Can a founder monitor the pilot?

Yes. Founder monitoring exists to track pilot organizations, listings, requests, transactions, certificates, trust, verification, feedback, and open issues.

## Verification

### 16. Why is verification required?

Verification creates trust before organizations exchange resources. It also helps control category eligibility and restricted workflows.

### 17. What verification documents are needed?

Document types can include trade license, VAT/TRN, food permit, storage permit, NGO license, government authorization, or other founder-approved evidence.

### 18. What are the verification levels?

Verification levels include Unverified, Basic Verified, Business Verified, Enterprise Verified, NGO Verified, and Government Verified.

### 19. Can an organization start before full verification?

For the controlled pilot, founder-approved manual clearance may be used for safe categories. Restricted or sensitive categories require stricter verification.

### 20. What happens if a document expires?

Expired documents reduce trust and may restrict access to certain workflows or categories until updated.

## Trust Score

### 21. What is the trust score?

Trust score is a 0-100 operational reputation score for organizations. It reflects verification, completed transactions, reliability, response behavior, cancellations, disputes, audit events, profile completeness, and document status.

### 22. What are the trust levels?

Trust levels are Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum.

### 23. Is trust score a public rating?

No. It is not a social review score. It is an operational confidence signal based on platform activity and verification state.

### 24. Can trust score change?

Yes. Trust score can change as organizations verify documents, complete transfers, cancel workflows, trigger disputes, or improve profile completeness.

### 25. Can a new organization have a high score?

Usually not immediately. New organizations need verification and workflow history before earning higher trust.

## Listings And Discovery

### 26. What can an organization list?

During the pilot, organizations should list approved surplus resources such as prepared food, packaged food, furniture, fixtures, pallets, cartons, packaging, bins, or community supplies.

### 27. What should not be listed?

The pilot should avoid hazardous, medical, highly regulated, legally sensitive, or unclear ownership resources unless founder-approved and properly verified.

### 28. Who can create a listing?

Listing creation should be limited to authorized organization roles, typically Organization Admins during the pilot.

### 29. Can recipients search and filter listings?

Yes. Discovery supports finding relevant resources and reviewing trust and verification context before making a request.

### 30. Can a listing be over-requested?

Quantity controls are designed to prevent over-allocation. The pilot specifically validates this through simulation and live staging checks.

## Request And Handover Workflow

### 31. How does requesting work?

A receiver requests a specific quantity and provides context. The sender reviews and accepts or declines.

### 32. Who approves requests?

The sender organization controls acceptance. In the pilot, the founder may assist the first request and handover.

### 33. What happens after acceptance?

The item is reserved for the accepted request, and the sender and receiver coordinate handover details.

### 34. How is handover confirmed?

Handover is confirmed through the completed transaction workflow. For the pilot, evidence and founder oversight may be used manually.

### 35. What if handover fails?

The issue should be logged immediately. Critical or repeated handover failures may pause the organization or the pilot workflow.

## Impact Dashboard

### 36. What impact does ReDist track?

ReDist tracks AED recovered, resources redistributed, waste prevented, estimated CO2 saved, completed transactions, active listings, active requests, top categories, and top locations.

### 37. Are impact calculations exact?

Impact calculations are estimates for pilot reporting. They should be treated as directional until category-specific calculation rules are refined with real data.

### 38. Who can see impact data?

Organizations see their own impact. Founder and authorized platform roles can review pilot/platform impact.

### 39. Why is AED recovered included?

AED recovered helps organizations understand operational value retained or redirected through redistribution.

### 40. Can impact reports be used externally?

Pilot impact reports should be reviewed carefully before external use. The platform can provide evidence, but final external claims should be approved by the organization.

## Transfer Certificates And QR Verification

### 41. What is a transfer certificate?

A transfer certificate is ReDist’s auditable proof that a redistribution transfer was recorded as completed.

### 42. When is a certificate generated?

It is generated after a completed transfer workflow.

### 43. What does the certificate include?

It includes certificate ID, sender, receiver, item summary, quantity, value, transfer date, location, handover method, trust snapshot, impact snapshot, and QR verification token.

### 44. Is the certificate a legal document?

No. It is platform proof of a recorded ReDist transfer. It is not a tax invoice, payment receipt, customs document, or legal title document.

### 45. What does QR verification show?

QR verification shows public-safe facts such as valid/invalid status, certificate ID, sender, receiver, transfer date, and resource summary.

### 46. Does QR verification expose private data?

No. It should not expose internal notes, private documents, contact details, or audit records.

## Security And Data

### 47. How is organization data protected?

ReDist uses role-based permissions, server-side checks, API protection, route protection, and multi-tenant isolation.

### 48. Can users bypass the UI and call APIs directly?

API access is also permission-checked server-side. The pilot validation includes unauthorized API access checks.

### 49. Is audit logging available?

Yes. Privileged actions and denied permission attempts are audit logged.

### 50. What happens if a security issue is reported?

Security issues override all pilot work. Critical issues pause the affected workflow or pilot expansion until resolved.
