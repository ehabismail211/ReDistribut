# ReDist Public FAQ

Date: 2026-06-22  
Status: Public FAQ draft  
Evidence boundary: ReDist is pilot-ready. Real pilot outcomes should not be claimed until completed and approved.

## General

### 1. What is ReDist?

ReDist is a UAE-first redistribution management platform for verified organizations. It helps organizations list surplus resources, discover available resources, submit requests, coordinate handovers, verify completion, and track impact evidence.

### 2. Is ReDist a marketplace?

ReDist is not designed as a consumer marketplace. It is an organization-to-organization redistribution workflow built around trust, approval, handover evidence, and impact visibility.

### 3. Who is ReDist for?

ReDist is for suppliers with usable surplus, recipients that need resources, administrators who manage verification, and partners who support circular economy activity.

### 4. Is ReDist live publicly?

ReDist is preparing founder-guided UAE pilot workflows. Public self-serve access is not the current launch mode.

### 5. What problem does ReDist solve?

ReDist helps organizations move from informal surplus coordination to a structured workflow with listings, requests, handovers, certificates, and impact tracking.

### 6. Why is ReDist UAE-first?

The UAE has strong hospitality, logistics, retail, NGO, and ESG activity. ReDist is starting there to validate a focused operating model before broader expansion.

### 7. What does "redistribution" mean?

Redistribution means moving usable surplus resources from one organization to another organization that can use them.

### 8. What does "surplus" mean?

Surplus means usable resources an organization no longer needs, has in excess, or wants to redirect before they become waste or idle inventory.

### 9. Is ReDist only for donations?

No. ReDist can support different redistribution models conceptually, but early pilot workflows should remain simple, controlled, and low-risk. Payments and escrow are not part of the early public promise.

### 10. Does ReDist handle delivery?

No. ReDist does not currently act as a delivery provider. Suppliers and recipients coordinate handover details.

## Suppliers

### 11. Who can become a supplier?

UAE organizations with usable surplus may apply for pilot consideration. Early supplier fit depends on category safety, responsible contact availability, and willingness to complete one guided workflow.

### 12. What can suppliers list?

Pilot-safe examples include furniture, fixtures, reusable warehouse materials, cartons, bins, non-sensitive equipment, and packaged non-perishable supplies.

### 13. Can restaurants list food?

Food-adjacent workflows may require additional compliance review. ReDist should not begin with prepared food unless safety, handling, and legal requirements are reviewed.

### 14. Can hotels list surplus furniture?

Yes, hotel furniture and fixtures are strong examples of practical early pilot categories if condition, quantity, and pickup details are clear.

### 15. Can warehouses list pallets or cartons?

Yes, reusable warehouse materials such as pallets, cartons, bins, and packaging are strong early pilot examples.

### 16. Does a supplier have to accept every request?

No. Suppliers review requests before approving or declining.

### 17. Can suppliers control quantity?

Yes. Suppliers should list available quantity and approve only the quantity they are willing to transfer.

### 18. Can suppliers see who is requesting resources?

The workflow is designed around verified organizations and request review. Supplier visibility should follow platform access and privacy rules.

### 19. What if a listed item is no longer available?

The supplier should update or close the listing and communicate clearly with any pending requesters.

### 20. What does a supplier receive after a transfer?

A supplier can receive operational evidence of the completed transfer, including certificate-backed confirmation where supported.

## Recipients

### 21. Who can become a recipient?

Recipients may include NGOs, charities, community organizations, schools, training centers, social enterprises, or approved organizations with real resource needs.

### 22. How do recipients find resources?

Recipients use Discover to view available surplus by practical details such as category, location, quantity, condition, urgency, and supplier context.

### 23. Can recipients request any resource?

Recipients can submit requests for available resources, but supplier approval and category eligibility still apply.

### 24. Does ReDist guarantee that requests are approved?

No. Suppliers review and decide whether to approve requests.

### 25. Can recipients request partial quantities?

The workflow is designed to support practical quantity decisions. For early pilot workflows, the founder may help clarify partial request handling.

### 26. Can recipients use ReDist to find free resources?

Some pilot resources may be free, but ReDist should not be described only as a free-goods platform. It is a redistribution workflow.

### 27. Can individuals request resources?

ReDist is designed for organization-to-organization workflows, not individual consumer requests.

### 28. What happens after a request is approved?

The workflow moves toward handover coordination, verification, certificate review, and impact tracking.

### 29. What if the resource condition is different from expected?

The issue should be logged and handled through the support or dispute process. Condition details should be clarified before handover.

### 30. Can recipients use certificates for reporting?

Certificates may support internal evidence, but they are operational records, not audited ESG certificates or legal documents.

## Trust and Verification

### 31. Are organizations verified?

ReDist is designed around verified organization workflows. Early pilot participation is founder-guided and controlled.

### 32. What does verification mean?

Verification may include organization identity, responsible contact, eligibility, and category review. It does not mean ReDist guarantees every outcome.

### 33. Does ReDist inspect every item?

No. ReDist is not an inspection service. Suppliers and recipients remain responsible for accurate descriptions and handover confirmation.

### 34. Does ReDist guarantee item quality?

No. ReDist does not guarantee item quality, suitability, safety, or legal compliance.

### 35. What is a ReDist certificate?

A ReDist certificate is operational evidence that a transfer workflow was completed in the platform.

### 36. Is a certificate a legal document?

No. It is not a legal guarantee, tax receipt, safety certificate, government approval, or audited ESG certificate.

### 37. Can certificates be verified?

ReDist is designed to support certificate verification and referenceable transfer evidence.

### 38. Can certificates be disputed?

Yes. Certificate disputes should be reviewed through the support and dispute process.

### 39. Does ReDist support audit logs?

ReDist has an audit logging framework and validation work, but public claims should stay within what has been implemented and validated.

### 40. How does ReDist protect organization boundaries?

ReDist is designed around role-based access, organization boundaries, and protected workflows. Security documents are framework guidance, not formal certification.

## Impact

### 41. What impact does ReDist track?

ReDist is designed to track value recovered, items redistributed, waste diverted, certificate evidence, and recipient usefulness.

### 42. Does ReDist have real impact results yet?

No public real pilot impact results are available yet. ReDist should publish results only after completed, verified workflows and participant approval.

### 43. Are CO2 metrics available?

Environmental estimates may be possible where defensible data exists, but they must be clearly labeled and should not be treated as audited claims.

### 44. Can organizations use ReDist for ESG reporting?

ReDist can support evidence gathering for ESG conversations, but formal ESG reporting requires appropriate methodology and review.

### 45. What is AED value recovered?

It is an estimate of the value preserved by redistributing usable resources. It should be based on completed transfers and labeled clearly.

### 46. Does ReDist count beneficiaries?

ReDist should not infer beneficiary counts unless a recipient provides and approves that information.

## Pilot and Launch

### 47. What is the current pilot status?

ReDist is prepared for founder-guided UAE Pilot Wave 1. The first real live organization workflows are pending.

### 48. How can an organization join the pilot?

Organizations can request a founder conversation and may be invited if they fit the pilot criteria.

### 49. What makes a good first pilot participant?

A good participant has one low-risk use case, one responsible contact, ability to complete a workflow within two weeks, and willingness to provide feedback.

### 50. What categories are not suitable for the first pilot?

Hazardous, medical, regulated, legally sensitive, unsafe, or unclear categories are not suitable for the first pilot.

### 51. Will ReDist be available outside the UAE?

The future roadmap may include GCC and regional expansion, but the current focus is UAE validation.

### 52. Does ReDist charge during the pilot?

The recommended early pilot model is founder-guided and free while value and willingness to pay are validated.

### 53. What are future paid options?

Future options may include organization subscriptions, enterprise packages, ESG reporting packages, and onboarding services. Pricing is not yet validated by paying customers.

### 54. Can partners sponsor a pilot?

Yes, strategic partners or ESG sponsors may support controlled pilot workflows, subject to founder review.

### 55. Can government entities partner with ReDist?

Government-related collaboration may be explored, but ReDist should not imply endorsement or approval unless formally granted.

## Contact

### 56. How do I contact ReDist?

Use the public contact form or founder contact path once available.

### 57. What should I include in an inquiry?

Include organization name, role, location, whether you are a supplier, recipient, or partner, and the resource category or use case.

### 58. Can I request a demo?

Qualified organizations and partners may request a founder-led walkthrough.

### 59. Can I access the platform immediately?

No. Pilot access is controlled and should be shared only after qualification.

### 60. What is the safest next step?

Request a conversation. ReDist can then determine whether your organization fits a controlled pilot workflow or partner discussion.
