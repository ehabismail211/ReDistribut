# ReDist Impact Model

Date: 2026-06-21  
Status: Impact framework for partners, ESG stakeholders, and future reporting  
Evidence level: Product capability and model design only. Real-world impact claims require completed verified transfers.

## Impact Principle

ReDist should measure impact from verified redistribution activity, not from marketing estimates alone.

The model should connect each impact claim to:

- A listed resource.
- A request.
- A completed handover.
- Verification evidence.
- Certificate reference.
- Impact calculation method.

## Waste Diversion

Waste diversion measures the resources moved into reuse instead of disposal, idleness, or unmanaged loss.

Potential measures:

- Count of redistributed items.
- Quantity by category.
- Estimated weight where available.
- Completed transfer count.
- Category-level diversion summaries.

Current evidence boundary:

- ReDist can display and organize waste diversion concepts.
- Real diversion totals should not be claimed until completed pilot transfers are recorded.
- Weight-based diversion should be labeled as estimated unless measured directly.

## AED Value Recovery

AED value recovery estimates the value preserved by redistributing usable resources.

Potential measures:

- Supplier-stated estimated value.
- Replacement value approximation.
- Category-level recovered value.
- Completed-transfer value only.

Recommended rule:

- Use completed verified transfers for official impact reporting.
- Separate active or pending opportunities from completed value.
- Label supplier-entered or estimated values as unaudited.

## Social Impact

Social impact describes how recipient organizations benefit from redistributed resources.

Potential measures:

- Recipient organizations served.
- Resources received by category.
- Operational usefulness reported by recipients.
- Beneficiary context where a recipient voluntarily provides it.

Evidence boundary:

- Do not infer beneficiary counts unless a recipient explicitly provides them.
- Do not claim charitable outcome beyond the verified handover.
- Use recipient-approved quotes and stories only after permission.

## Environmental Impact

Environmental impact may include CO2 or equivalent estimates where reliable category factors are available.

Recommended approach:

- Start with conservative category-level estimates.
- Clearly label the calculation method.
- Avoid audited ESG language unless reviewed by qualified professionals.
- Keep environmental metrics secondary to verified transfer evidence until methods mature.

Current boundary:

- CO2 impact can be modeled, but should be marked as estimated.
- Formal ESG reporting requires professional review and stronger methodology.

## Trust Framework

The ReDist impact model depends on trust controls:

- Verified organizations.
- Role-based access.
- Request and approval workflow.
- Handover verification.
- Certificate references.
- Audit and admin review concepts.
- Founder-guided pilot oversight.

The certificate is not a legal guarantee, audited ESG certificate, tax receipt, or safety certification. It is operational evidence that a transfer workflow was completed inside ReDist.

## Impact Evidence Stack

| Evidence Layer | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Listing | Shows what resource was offered |
| Request | Shows recipient intent and requested quantity |
| Approval | Shows supplier decision |
| Transfer status | Shows handover progress |
| Verification | Shows completion signal |
| Certificate | Provides referenceable proof |
| Impact summary | Aggregates completed evidence |
| Founder observation | Captures early pilot learnings |

## Reporting Guidance

Use these categories in partner and ESG materials:

- Verified transfer count.
- Redistributed value.
- Items redistributed.
- Estimated waste diverted.
- Certificate-backed evidence.
- Recipient usefulness.
- Pilot impact summary.

Do not use:

- Audited impact claims without audit.
- Guaranteed environmental savings.
- Unsupported beneficiary numbers.
- Public success stories without participant approval.
