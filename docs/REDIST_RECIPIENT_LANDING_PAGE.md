## Who This Page Is For

ReDist is for organizations that can make practical use of surplus resources, including:

- NGOs and charities.
- Community support organizations.
- Schools and training centers.
- Social enterprises.
- Nonprofit programs.
- Approved organizational recipients.

## Recipient Problem

Useful resources can be hard to find through informal channels.

Recipient questions:

- What resources are available?
- Where are they located?
- What quantity can we request?
- Is the supplier verified?
- Is the resource suitable?
- What is the handover status?
- Can we keep proof after completion?

## Recipient Value Proposition

ReDist helps recipients:

- Discover available surplus.
- Understand quantity, condition, location, and urgency.
- Submit structured requests.
- Track request and transfer status.
- Coordinate handover details.
- Receive certificate-backed completion evidence.
- Show resource receipt and usefulness to internal stakeholders or partners.

## What Recipients Can Request

Pilot-safe examples may include:

- Furniture.
- Shelving.
- Storage bins.
- Cartons and packaging.
- Non-sensitive equipment.
- Training supplies.
- Packaged non-perishable resources.

Category note:

Some categories may require additional review before they can be requested or transferred. ReDist will begin with low-risk workflows during the controlled pilot.

## Recipient Workflow

1. Confirm organization eligibility.
2. Discover available resources.
3. Submit a request.
4. Wait for supplier review.
5. Coordinate handover if approved.
6. Verify receipt.
7. Review certificate and impact evidence.

## Trust and Evidence

ReDist is designed around organization-to-organization redistribution.

Recipients can see practical details before requesting:

- Resource description.
- Supplier organization.
- Quantity.
- Location.
- Condition.
- Urgency.
- Handover notes.

After completion, certificates can provide operational evidence of the transfer.

## Pilot Invitation

The ideal first recipient:

- Has a real resource need.
- Can assign one responsible contact.
- Can coordinate pickup or handover.
- Can provide feedback within 48 hours.
- Understands that certificates are operational evidence, not legal or audited ESG documents.
