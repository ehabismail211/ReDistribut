# ReDist Customer Onboarding Framework

Date: 2026-06-21  
Status: Commercial onboarding framework; to be validated after Wave 1

## Overview

ReDist onboarding should move prospects through six stages:

Lead -> Demo -> Pilot -> Activation -> Adoption -> Expansion

The framework should remain founder-guided until real pilot evidence proves which steps can become self-serve.

## Stage 1: Lead

Goal: Identify organizations with a real surplus or resource need.

Lead sources:

- Founder network.
- Hospitality groups.
- NGO/charity networks.
- Warehouses and distributors.
- Sustainability and ESG leaders.
- Government-related circular economy contacts.
- Partner referrals.

Qualification criteria:

- UAE organization context.
- Clear surplus or resource need.
- Safe, permitted category.
- Named owner/contact.
- Ability to complete one guided workflow.
- Willingness to provide feedback.

Lead exit criteria:

- Prospect agrees to a short fit call.
- Founder records likely participant type and use case.

## Stage 2: Demo

Goal: Show ReDist as an operational workflow, not a generic marketplace.

Demo flow:

1. Problem: surplus and resource needs are fragmented.
2. Workflow: Dashboard -> Discover -> Requests -> Transfers -> Impact.
3. Supplier path: create listing, review request, verify handover.
4. Recipient path: discover resource, submit request, track transfer.
5. Evidence: certificate and impact summary.
6. Pilot ask: one guided workflow and feedback.

Demo exit criteria:

- Prospect confirms the workflow is relevant.
- First use case is identified.
- Category is safe for pilot/commercial stage.
- Decision maker or operational owner is known.

## Stage 3: Pilot

Goal: Validate one real workflow with founder support.

Pilot steps:

- Invite organization.
- Confirm contact and category.
- Complete onboarding call.
- Set up account/workspace.
- Run first listing or request.
- Complete request/approval/handover where possible.
- Review certificate and impact.
- Capture feedback.

Pilot exit criteria:

- First workflow completed or blocked with clear reason.
- Feedback captured.
- Issue log updated.
- Decision made: continue, convert, defer, or stop.

## Stage 4: Activation

Goal: Convert a successful pilot participant into a repeat user or paying account.

Activation steps:

- Confirm ongoing use case.
- Select pricing tier.
- Confirm account owner.
- Confirm billing process.
- Confirm support channel.
- Set usage goals for first 30 days.
- Review restrictions and acceptable use.

Activation metrics:

- First paid agreement or founder-approved trial extension.
- At least one repeat listing/request.
- No unresolved Critical issues.
- User knows where to start in Dashboard.

## Stage 5: Adoption

Goal: Make ReDist part of repeat operations.

Adoption activities:

- Weekly or biweekly check-ins.
- Review active listings, requests, transfers, and certificates.
- Capture support issues.
- Train additional users.
- Review impact summary.
- Identify expansion use cases.

Adoption metrics:

- Repeat monthly usage.
- More than one user active.
- Certificates reviewed by stakeholder.
- Satisfaction 4/5 or higher.
- Support load manageable.

## Stage 6: Expansion

Goal: Expand to more branches, categories, users, or reporting packages.

Expansion triggers:

- Multiple successful transfers.
- More branches request access.
- ESG/sustainability team asks for reporting.
- Operations wants repeat workflow.
- Recipient/supplier network asks for more participants.

Expansion options:

- Upgrade to Professional.
- Add ESG reporting package.
- Add branches.
- Add users.
- Move to Enterprise discovery.
- Start integration discovery.

Expansion exit criteria:

- Contract and billing updated.
- Support scope confirmed.
- Implementation plan documented.
- Risks reviewed.

## Customer Health Signals

| Signal | Healthy | At Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Workflow completion | Listings, requests, transfers complete monthly | Workflow stalls after onboarding |
| Trust | Participants understand certificate and verification | Participants are unsure what evidence means |
| Support | Questions decrease after first workflow | Founder must guide every step repeatedly |
| Value | Impact/certificate evidence is shared internally | No internal owner for value |
| Expansion | More users or branches ask to join | Primary contact disengages |

## Recommended First Paying Customer Profile

Best first paying profile:

- UAE restaurant or hotel group.
- 2-3 locations.
- Recurring safe surplus.
- Named sustainability or operations owner.
- Interested in certificate-backed impact evidence.
- Can complete founder-guided onboarding.
- Does not require payments, escrow, deep integration, or audited ESG claims immediately.

Why:

- Clear supplier-side pain.
- Repeatable surplus.
- ESG and waste reduction narrative.
- Multi-location expansion potential.
- Operational buyer can see value quickly.
