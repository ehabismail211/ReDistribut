# UAE Pilot Participant Selection Guide

Date: 2026-06-21  
Purpose: Select a small, balanced Wave 1 cohort that can validate supply, demand, handover, certificate, and impact workflows with manageable operational risk.

## Selection Principles

Wave 1 should prioritize learning quality over participant volume.

Select organizations that:

- Have a real surplus or resource need.
- Can assign an accountable operations contact.
- Can participate in a founder-guided workflow.
- Can provide feedback within 48 hours.
- Operate in permitted launch categories.
- Can complete a simple handover without complex legal or regulated goods concerns.

Avoid organizations that:

- Require unmanaged self-serve onboarding.
- Need payment, escrow, customs, or tax-invoice automation.
- Deal mainly in restricted goods.
- Cannot confirm item condition or handover responsibility.
- Cannot identify a primary pilot owner.

## Supplier Profile Requirements

Ideal Wave 1 suppliers:

- UAE-registered organization.
- Has non-regulated surplus inventory.
- Can verify basic business identity.
- Has a named owner/admin and inventory contact.
- Can publish a listing with accurate quantity, location, urgency, and handover method.
- Can respond to requests within the same business day.
- Can participate in first handover verification.

Preferred supplier types:

- Restaurant or food operator with short-window surplus.
- Hotel with furniture, fixtures, or operating supplies.
- Warehouse or distributor with packaging, pallets, or bulk stock.
- Retail or manufacturer with slow-moving non-regulated stock.

Supplier exclusion criteria:

- Medical, pharmaceutical, hazardous, controlled, or restricted goods.
- Unclear ownership of items.
- No reliable handover contact.
- No ability to describe item condition.
- Requires payment processing inside ReDist during Wave 1.

## Recipient Profile Requirements

Ideal Wave 1 recipients:

- UAE-registered organization or approved community partner.
- Has a real need for available resource types.
- Can request resources through a guided workflow.
- Can confirm quantity needed and pickup/delivery readiness.
- Can review certificate and impact evidence after handover.
- Can provide structured feedback.

Preferred recipient types:

- NGO or community organization.
- School, community program, or institutional recipient.
- Small business with legitimate reuse need.
- Packaging, logistics, or operations partner that can reuse bulk materials.

Recipient exclusion criteria:

- Individual consumer account without organization context.
- Requests for restricted goods.
- No ability to confirm receipt.
- No clear operational use for requested resources.
- No named pilot participant.

## Recommended First 5 Organizations

| Order | Type | Suggested Profile | Pilot Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Restaurant supplier | Dubai restaurant group with prepared meal surplus | Validate urgent food listing, request, approval, same-day handover |
| 2 | NGO recipient | Dubai or Sharjah community support organization | Validate recipient discovery, request, receipt confirmation, impact story |
| 3 | Hotel supplier | Abu Dhabi or Dubai hotel with furniture/fixtures surplus | Validate slower handover and non-food resource workflow |
| 4 | Warehouse supplier | Jebel Ali or Sharjah warehouse with pallets/cartons | Validate bulk quantity, partial reservations, logistics handover |
| 5 | School or community recipient | School/community program needing furniture, meals, or supplies | Validate recipient-side usability and certificate interpretation |

Optional alternate:

- Packaging supplier or manufacturer if warehouse scenario is easier to execute.

## Cohort Mix

Minimum Wave 1 mix:

- 2 suppliers.
- 2 recipients.
- 1 mixed participant or operational partner.
- At least 1 urgent listing.
- At least 1 bulk listing.
- At least 1 non-food listing.
- At least 1 certificate-generating completed handover.

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Participant expects self-serve product | Medium | Medium | Communicate founder-guided pilot scope before invite |
| Supplier lists restricted goods | Low | High | Founder approves categories before first listing |
| Food handover creates safety concern | Medium | High | Require food permit/manual review and same-day pickup details |
| Quantity mismatch at handover | Medium | Medium | Confirm quantity before approval and at handover |
| Recipient cannot collect on time | Medium | Medium | Use Transfers workspace and support channel for coordination |
| Certificate data is incorrect | Low | High | Founder reviews certificate after each first transfer |
| Impact claims misunderstood | Medium | Medium | Explain estimates vs completed-transfer evidence |
| Admin controls confuse pilot participant | Medium | Medium | Keep founder/admin sections internal during sessions |

## Selection Recommendation

Proceed with a balanced 5-organization cohort only if both supply and demand sides are confirmed before Day 1. Do not start Wave 1 with suppliers only or recipients only.
