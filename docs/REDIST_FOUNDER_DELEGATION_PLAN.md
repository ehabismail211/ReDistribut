# ReDist Founder Delegation Plan

Date: 2026-06-22  
Status: Founder workload reduction plan  
Purpose: Reduce founder capacity risk while preserving strategic control.

## Delegation Principle

The founder should keep high-judgment decisions and delegate repeatable operating tasks as soon as volume makes them predictable.

The founder should not become the permanent support desk, operations coordinator, verification clerk, and escalation owner at the same time.

## Founder Keeps

The founder should continue to own:

- Pilot admission decisions.
- Final approval for risky categories.
- Strategic partner and commercial conversations.
- Pricing and contract direction.
- Legal, governance, and reputation decisions.
- Investor and sponsor conversations.
- Final Go / Conditional Go / No-Go calls.
- Critical trust, safety, privacy, or certificate escalations.
- Post-mortem review for first real transfers.

## Founder Delegates

Delegate to Operations Lead:

- Daily tracker updates.
- Follow-up reminders.
- Meeting scheduling.
- Onboarding preparation.
- Handover coordination.
- Issue log maintenance.
- Weekly operations draft.

Delegate to Verification Reviewer:

- Routine document checklist review.
- Organization profile completeness checks.
- Category eligibility preparation.
- Verification notes.
- Certificate data consistency checks.

Delegate to Customer Success:

- Supplier onboarding calls.
- Recipient onboarding calls.
- Routine support questions.
- Feedback form collection.
- FAQ updates.
- Repeated confusion logging.

Delegate to Engineering Owner:

- Validation runs.
- Deployment maintenance.
- Critical bug investigation.
- Environment configuration.
- Technical incident response.

## Founder Eliminates

The founder should stop doing or avoid starting:

- Manual spreadsheet duplication when a tracker already exists.
- Custom one-off onboarding for every participant after scripts stabilize.
- Building features because prospects are quiet.
- Accepting high-risk categories to create momentum.
- Re-explaining certificate limits without improving the standard script.
- Running multiple pilot transfers in parallel before the first real transfer is reviewed.
- Treating simulated results as external traction.

## Delegation Timeline

| Stage | Founder Role | Delegation Target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Founder-only | Own everything, but document repeated tasks | None |
| First 2-5 organizations | Keep strategy and final approvals | Operations & Customer Success Lead |
| 5-15 organizations | Keep exceptions and commercial direction | Verification Reviewer for routine review |
| 15-25 organizations | Keep escalation and growth direction | Customer Success for onboarding/support |
| 25+ organizations | GM role, not daily operator | Operations pod plus engineering owner |

## Founder Workload Assessment

Current workload risk: Medium-High.

Why:

- ReDist has many operating artifacts and a validated staging environment.
- The founder can run the first guided pilot.
- Virtual Pilot Wave 1 showed that parallel workflows can overload manual founder monitoring.
- Outreach, onboarding, verification, support, transfer monitoring, and post-mortems will compete for attention.

Immediate control:

- Run only one active transfer at a time until the first real transfer post-mortem is complete.
- Use docs and trackers as operating tools, not optional references.
- Hire or assign operations support before expanding beyond 2-5 active organizations.
