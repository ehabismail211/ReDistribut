# ReDist Commercial Model

Date: 2026-06-21  
Status: Strategy estimate; not validated by real paying customers yet  
Scope: UAE-first commercial planning after founder-guided pilot evidence

## Executive Summary

ReDist should commercialize as an enterprise circular redistribution operating platform, not as a consumer marketplace.

The strongest initial revenue path is subscription-led:

- Organization subscriptions for verified suppliers and recipients.
- Enterprise packages for multi-location groups.
- ESG/reporting add-ons for organizations that need certificate-backed evidence.
- Optional services for onboarding, verification support, and pilot operations.

Do not introduce transaction fees, payments, escrow, or success fees until legal, operational, and compliance risk is separately assessed.

## Revenue Model

Recommended revenue streams:

| Revenue Stream | Description | Timing | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Organization subscription | Monthly or annual access for verified organizations | Launch phase | Core recurring revenue |
| Enterprise subscription | Multi-branch access, governance, priority support, reporting | Growth / Enterprise phase | Best fit for hotel groups, retail groups, warehouses |
| ESG reporting package | Certificate-backed impact summaries and executive reports | Growth phase | High perceived value if impact evidence is credible |
| Pilot setup fee | Optional founder-led onboarding package for larger pilots | Pilot / Launch phase | Use carefully; avoid charging before value is clear |
| Verification support fee | Assisted document review and organization readiness | Launch phase | Should not override operational trust controls |
| Integration / API fee | Inventory, ERP, procurement, ESG tool integrations | Enterprise phase | Defer until product and contracts mature |
| Success fee | Fee tied to completed transfers or recovered value | Later only | Requires legal/commercial review; not Wave 1 |

## Phase 1: Pilot Phase

Goal: Validate willingness to use ReDist before charging.

Commercial posture:

- Free guided pilot for the first small cohort.
- No self-serve public pricing.
- No payments inside ReDist.
- No success fees.
- Founder manually qualifies every participant.
- Capture value, workflow, trust, and reporting feedback.

Commercial questions to answer:

- Which organization type feels the pain most urgently?
- Which participant gets the clearest measurable value?
- Does certificate evidence matter enough to pay for?
- Does impact reporting influence budget owner interest?
- Who would own the subscription internally: operations, sustainability, procurement, CSR, or finance?

Pilot phase exit criteria:

- At least 2 complete supplier-recipient workflows.
- Certificate reviewed positively by both sides.
- No Critical trust, safety, or data-boundary issue.
- At least 2 organizations say they would continue using ReDist.
- Founder can identify the most likely paying buyer.

## Phase 2: Launch Phase

Goal: Convert pilot learning into first paid organization accounts.

Recommended commercial motion:

- Offer Starter and Professional organization tiers.
- Keep onboarding founder-assisted.
- Use annual contracts where possible.
- Keep pricing simple and transparent.
- Sell workflow and evidence, not volume alone.

Target buyers:

- Restaurant groups with recurring surplus.
- Hotels with operating supply/furniture surplus.
- Warehouses with reusable bulk materials.
- NGOs/charities that need proof of received resources.
- Sustainability/CSR teams that need evidence-backed reporting.

Launch phase product promises:

- Verified organization workspace.
- Listing/request/transfer workflow.
- Certificate-backed transfer proof.
- Basic impact dashboard.
- Founder/priority onboarding support.

Launch phase exclusions:

- Payment processing.
- Escrow.
- Delivery-provider management.
- Legal/tax donation claims.
- Regulated goods without a separate compliance process.
- Unrestricted public self-serve onboarding.

## Phase 3: Growth Phase

Goal: Grow from individual organizations to repeatable multi-organization workflows.

Commercial motion:

- Professional plans become the default.
- Add ESG reporting packages.
- Expand to multi-branch groups.
- Formalize customer success and support SLAs.
- Introduce quarterly business reviews for larger accounts.
- Build partner-led channels through hospitality, sustainability, NGO, and logistics networks.

Growth phase revenue mix:

- 60-70% organization subscriptions.
- 15-25% enterprise/group subscriptions.
- 10-20% ESG/reporting packages and services.
- 0-10% integration or special project fees.

Growth phase requirements:

- Clear contracts and acceptable use policies.
- Support process and response targets.
- Account lifecycle and billing operations.
- Data retention and privacy terms.
- Reporting disclaimers for impact estimates.
- Operational monitoring and reliability process.

## Phase 4: Enterprise Phase

Goal: Serve large organizations with governance, reporting, multi-branch operations, and integrations.

Enterprise customers may include:

- Hotel groups.
- Restaurant groups.
- Retail groups.
- Warehouse and logistics operators.
- Government-related entities.
- ESG-driven corporate groups.

Enterprise commercial motion:

- Annual contracts.
- Paid onboarding.
- Multi-branch pricing.
- Dedicated success support.
- Custom reporting pack.
- Security and data processing review.
- Integration discovery and paid implementation where justified.

Enterprise revenue streams:

- Platform subscription.
- Branch/location expansion fees.
- ESG/reporting package.
- Implementation fee.
- Premium support fee.
- Integration fee.

Enterprise phase readiness gates:

- MSA/DPA/SLA templates.
- Security review packet.
- Branch/location governance.
- Role granularity review.
- Audit export or reporting workflow.
- Commercial invoicing and renewal process.

## Pricing Principles

- Price for operational proof, trust, and impact evidence.
- Do not price like a consumer listing marketplace.
- Keep entry pricing low enough for first adoption.
- Charge more for multi-location complexity and reporting value.
- Avoid per-transfer fees until legal/commercial risk is clear.
- Use annual discounts to reduce churn and manual billing load.

## Commercial Readiness Risks

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Real pilot data not available yet | High | Treat all pricing as estimates until Wave 1 feedback |
| Legal/compliance terms incomplete for paid launch | High | Prepare MSA, pilot agreement, DPA, acceptable use, disclaimers |
| Impact claims may be interpreted as audited ESG proof | High | Label impact metrics as estimates unless audited |
| Payment and billing operations not implemented | Medium | Use manual invoice process initially |
| Enterprise branch/role depth may be insufficient | Medium | Limit first enterprise use to founder-managed accounts |
| Support load unknown | Medium | Keep early customer count low and guided |
