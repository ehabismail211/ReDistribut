# ReDist Terms Of Service Framework

Date: 2026-06-21  
Status: Framework only; not legal advice  
Required review: Professional UAE legal review before commercial use

## Purpose

This document outlines the commercial Terms of Service areas ReDist should prepare before paid UAE launch.

It is not a legal document. It is a product and operations framework to help the founder and legal counsel draft formal terms.

## Legal Review Required

Professional UAE legal review is required for:

- Final Terms of Service.
- Liability limitations.
- Category restrictions.
- Food/resource safety language.
- Data protection language.
- Dispute handling language.
- Certificate disclaimers.
- Payment, invoicing, tax, and refund terms if commercial billing is introduced.

## Scope Of Service

ReDist provides a platform for verified or pilot-approved organizations to:

- List approved surplus resources.
- Discover available resources.
- Submit and review requests.
- Coordinate handover workflows.
- Record transfer completion.
- Generate certificate-backed platform evidence.
- View impact estimates and transfer history.

ReDist is not currently positioned as:

- A logistics provider.
- A payment processor.
- An escrow provider.
- A tax advisor.
- A legal title transfer service.
- A food safety certifier.
- A customs broker.
- A government regulator.

## User Responsibilities

Users should be responsible for:

- Providing accurate account information.
- Using only authorized organization accounts.
- Keeping login credentials secure.
- Following their organization's internal approval procedures.
- Submitting accurate listing, request, handover, and feedback information.
- Reporting errors, unsafe content, or suspicious activity promptly.
- Not sharing staging or production access outside approved users.
- Not attempting to access another organization's data.

## Organization Responsibilities

Organizations should be responsible for:

- Ensuring users are authorized representatives.
- Keeping organization profile and contact details accurate.
- Ensuring listed resources are owned or controlled by the organization.
- Ensuring listed/requested resources are lawful and safe for redistribution.
- Providing accurate condition, quantity, expiry, location, and handover details.
- Verifying recipient/supplier suitability according to internal policy.
- Maintaining any required licenses, permits, approvals, or internal authorizations.
- Complying with applicable UAE laws and sector-specific obligations.
- Preserving any external legal, financial, tax, or compliance records they require.

## Platform Limitations

Terms should clearly state that ReDist:

- Facilitates workflow and evidence, but does not guarantee the quality, safety, legality, or suitability of resources.
- Does not independently inspect every resource.
- Does not guarantee that a supplier or recipient will complete a handover.
- Does not replace organization due diligence.
- Does not provide tax, legal, safety, or regulatory advice.
- Does not certify impact metrics as audited ESG results unless separately agreed and validated.
- May suspend or restrict accounts, categories, listings, requests, certificates, or access where risk is identified.

## Transfer Responsibility Model

The transfer responsibility model should explain:

| Area | Responsibility Concept |
| --- | --- |
| Resource accuracy | Supplier provides accurate details and confirms right to transfer |
| Request accuracy | Recipient provides accurate request quantity, purpose, and readiness |
| Approval | Supplier decides whether to accept, decline, or request clarification |
| Handover | Supplier and recipient coordinate and complete handover according to agreed terms |
| Condition and suitability | Parties confirm condition and suitability before completion |
| Evidence | Parties provide accurate verification information where required |
| Completion | ReDist records completion based on workflow actions and evidence available |
| External obligations | Parties remain responsible for legal, tax, safety, and compliance obligations |

Professional legal review must define final liability allocation.

## Verification Limitations

Terms should explain that verification:

- Indicates that certain organization information or documents were reviewed.
- Does not guarantee future conduct.
- Does not guarantee resource quality or handover success.
- May be incomplete, expired, suspended, or revoked.
- May be risk-based and category-specific.
- Does not replace recipient/supplier due diligence.

Verification should not be marketed as legal certification without legal approval.

## Certificate Limitations

Terms should state that transfer certificates:

- Are platform evidence of a recorded ReDist transfer workflow.
- Are not tax invoices.
- Are not payment receipts.
- Are not customs documents.
- Are not legal title documents.
- Are not government certificates.
- May include impact estimates based on platform methodology.
- May be corrected, revoked, or marked disputed if material errors are found.

## Dispute Handling Concepts

Terms should define dispute concepts for:

- Incorrect listing details.
- Inaccurate quantity.
- Failed handover.
- Condition mismatch.
- Unauthorized user action.
- Certificate data dispute.
- Impact data dispute.
- Verification/document dispute.

Conceptual process:

1. Dispute is reported.
2. ReDist records the issue and preserves relevant audit evidence.
3. Affected workflow may be paused.
4. Founder/admin reviews available information.
5. Parties may be asked to provide clarification.
6. Certificate may be held, corrected, marked disputed, or revoked.
7. ReDist may close, escalate, suspend, or restrict access depending on severity.

Professional legal review must define enforceable rights, remedies, and limitations.

## Account Suspension Concepts

Potential suspension triggers:

- Cross-organization access attempt.
- False organization identity.
- Unsafe or restricted resource listing.
- Repeated failed handovers.
- Certificate or evidence manipulation.
- Harassment, abuse, fraud, or misuse.
- Legal or regulatory concern.
- Non-payment after commercial launch.
