# ReDist Operations Manual

Date: 2026-06-21  
Status: Founder-operated operations handbook  
Scope: UAE founder-guided pilot through early commercial readiness

## Purpose

This manual is the master operations handbook for running ReDist day to day.

It consolidates the practical operating model across:

- Pilot participants.
- Verification.
- Listings and requests.
- Transfers and certificates.
- Impact evidence.
- Issues and disputes.
- Security incidents.
- Customer support.
- Admin review.
- Governance and launch gates.

## Operating Model

### Phase 1: Founder-Operated Model

The founder owns all operating decisions until real workflows prove repeatability.

Founder responsibilities:

- Approve pilot participants.
- Approve first categories and use cases.
- Guide first organization onboarding.
- Review verification evidence.
- Monitor requests, transfers, certificates, and issues.
- Decide whether to continue, expand, or pause.
- Maintain the tracking center, issue log, metrics, and post-mortems.
- Prevent public/commercial expansion before launch blockers are closed.

### Phase 2: Future Team Scaling Model

As volume grows, responsibilities should split into:

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Operations Lead | Daily workflow health, issue routing, participant coordination |
| Verification Reviewer | Organization verification, document review, category eligibility |
| Customer Support Specialist | Supplier/recipient support, onboarding questions, issue triage |
| Trust & Safety Owner | Disputes, misuse, restricted categories, escalations |
| Engineering Owner | Security, incidents, deployment, data integrity, monitoring |
| Founder / GM | Governance, commercial decisions, enterprise escalation |

## Daily Operations Loop

Every operating day:

1. Review Dashboard and action queues.
2. Review pending verification items.
3. Review active requests and transfers.
4. Review certificate status and any certificate disputes.
5. Review issue log for Critical/High issues.
6. Update participant tracking center.
7. Confirm next actions and owners.

## Weekly Operations Loop

Every week:

1. Review pilot/commercial metrics.
2. Review risks and blockers.
3. Review participant progress and feedback.
4. Review unresolved issues and escalations.
5. Review verification and certificate evidence.
6. Decide continue, expand, pause, or remediate.

## Severity Model

| Severity | Meaning | Required Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Critical | Security, privacy, unsafe category, certificate integrity, tenant exposure | Pause affected workflow immediately |
| High | Blocks transfer, verification, certificate, or participant trust | Assign same day and track to closure |
| Medium | Workflow confusion or support issue that does not block safe operation | Review within 2 business days |
| Low | Copy, training, or polish issue | Review weekly |

## Operating Principles

- Do not expand before evidence.
- Do not treat certificates as legal/tax/government documents.
- Do not treat impact estimates as audited ESG claims.
- Do not store sensitive documents in public locations.
- Do not ignore small confusion points during pilot.
- Log issues before fixing or explaining them away.
- Preserve evidence for disputes.
- Keep founder-guided controls until support load is understood.

## Recommended Operations Structure

Current structure:

- Founder as Operations Lead.
- Founder as Customer Success.
- Engineering owner for technical/security incidents.
- Manual reviewer process for verification.

Next structure:

- Hire one Operations & Customer Success Lead.
- Founder retains governance and enterprise/commercial decisions.
- Engineering remains owner for security, data, and deployment incidents.

## Recommended First Operations Hire

Recommended first hire: Operations & Customer Success Lead.

Profile:

- UAE operations or hospitality/logistics experience.
- Strong customer communication.
- Comfortable with verification/document checklists.
- Able to coordinate suppliers and recipients.
- Can maintain issue logs, tracking center, and weekly reviews.
- Understands escalation discipline.

Why this hire first:

- ReDist's early bottleneck is not feature volume; it is guided execution, participant support, verification discipline, and issue closure.
